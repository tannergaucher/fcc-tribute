Timeline

  • 1914 March 28- Born in Brno, then Austria-Hungary
  • 1919 Moved to the small town of Nymburk where stepfather becomes a brewery manager.
  • 1920 Began primary school in Nymburk
  • 1925 Attended and failed first year of grammar school in Brno
  • 1934 June- Left school with certificate stating he could be considered for a place in a technical university course.
  • 1935 Registered at Charles University in Prague, faculty of law.
  • 1948 first collection of lyrical poetry (Ztracená ulička). Later withdrawn by communist regime.
  • 1959 first book withdrawn week before publication. Eventually published in 1963 as Perlička na dně (Pearl on the Bottom). Became a professional writer in this year.
  • 1964 Taneční hodiny pro starší a pokročilé (Dancing Lessons for the Advanced in Age).
  • 1965Ostře sledované vlaky (Closely Observed Trains)
  • 1965 Bought a country cottage in Kersko, near Nymburk. Home to his many cats.
  • 1968 Banned from publishing after Warsaw Pact Invasion
  • 1970Two books were banned after printing and before distribution.
  • 1975 Gave an interview making 'self-critical comments. Enabling him to publish again.
  • 1987 wife Eliška died.
  • 1994 Metting with Czech Presiden Valcav Havel, American Presiden Bill Clinton, and then UN ambassador Madeline Albright at U zlatého tygra, his favorite pub.
  • 1997 February - Died after falling from his 5th floor hospital window while attempting to feed pigeons.
